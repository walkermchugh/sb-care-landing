Prepared for: SafeBeat leadership  
Date: 2026-03-19  
Scope: Landing page strategy for SafeBeat Care (service-delivery/IDTF commercialization motion)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

SafeBeat has a strong strategic wedge that most cardiac competitors do not clearly own: **therapeutic monitoring for antiarrhythmic initiation/management** (not just diagnostic rhythm monitoring or CIED triage). The highest-leverage landing page strategy is to make that wedge explicit in the first screen, then quickly prove three things:

1. **Clinical legitimacy** (FDA/regulatory status, clinical workflow fit, physician trust)
2. **Operational/financial upside** (reduced inpatient burden + billable remote management)
3. **Low-friction implementation** (fast onboarding, minimal staff burden, clear next step)

The market’s current websites over-index on generic “AI cardiac monitoring” language. The opening for SafeBeat is to own a sharper narrative:

- "from mandatory inpatient loading to protocolized home-based therapeutic monitoring"
- "designed for antiarrhythmic workflow, not retrofitted from diagnostic/CIED tooling"

## 5. Industry-Standard Landing Page Architecture (for this category)

For B2B cardiology service + software hybrids, standard high-performing architecture should include:

1. **Hero with explicit problem-solution fit**
2. **Immediate trust strip** (regulatory, institutional, network logos)

4. **Economic/operations section** (capacity, staffing, revenue/cost)

8. **FAQ and risk/compliance clarity**

## 8. Recommended Landing Page Blueprint (Section-by-Section)

## 8.1 Hero

- Headline: therapeutic specificity, not generic AI
- Subhead: clinical + operational outcome in one sentence
- Primary CTA: "Schedule Clinical + Operations Demo"
- Secondary CTA: "Download Protocol Overview"
- Trust strip below CTA

## 8.2 Problem framing (quant + workflow pain)

- Current pathway friction and constraints
- Why this affects both access and practice operations
- Keep language plain; reserve dense terminology for expandable sections

## 8.3 How it works (4-step visual)

1. Identify eligible patient cohort
2. Enroll with supported device/protocol
3. Remote ECG/QT review with defined escalation rules
4. Clinician decision + documentation/billing workflow

## 8.4 Evidence and trust

- FDA/regulatory statement (precise, counsel-approved)
- NIH/clinical study references
- physician/institution endorsements
- "trusted by" logos where contractually permitted

## 8.5 Economics + operations

- Capacity impact (bed utilization narrative)
- staffing/time model
- reimbursement operations summary (without overpromising)
- CTA to ROI consult

## 8.6 Role tabs

- "For Electrophysiology Teams"
- "For Practice & Network Leaders"

## 8.7 Implementation

- timeline by week
- required people/resources
- compliance/data security highlights

## 8.8 FAQ

Include high-friction buyer questions:

- patient selection criteria
- safety oversight and escalation
- data governance/HIPAA posture
- integration model
- contracting / rollout model

## 9. Conversion and UX Rules You Should Enforce

1. One primary CTA language across entire page.
2. Repeat CTA after Hero, after Evidence, and in Footer.
3. Keep form short for first touch (name, organization, role, email, optional phone).
4. Add immediate trust cues near form submit button.
5. Use plain-language headings; keep advanced terms in supporting copy.
6. Keep mobile performance tight; buyers and physician champions will view on phones.

---

## 10. Compliance Guardrails for Copywriting

Before publishing any claim set:

1. Confirm cleared vs investigational language by product/module.
2. Ensure all efficacy/safety claims map to evidence you can produce.
3. Avoid implied FDA endorsement framing.
4. Avoid universal claims ("always," "proven for all patients") unless fully substantiated.
5. Add clear intended-use and limitations statement where required.

References:

- Investigational device promotion limitations: [21 CFR 812.7](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/21/812.7)
- FDA misbranding principles: [FDA labeling/misbranding page](https://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/general-device-labeling-requirements/labeling-requirements-misbranding)

## 11. 30-Day Execution Plan (Nontechnical Founder Version)

## Week 1: Positioning + claims lock

- finalize audience priority and CTA
- legal/regulatory review of claim language
- decide final trust proof set (logos, endorsements, citations)

## Week 2: Design and build

- choose template/platform (Framer recommended for speed)
- implement section architecture above
- write copy by proof hierarchy

## Week 3: Asset completion + instrumentation

- build one downloadable artifact (protocol overview or payer/admin brief)
- connect CRM + calendar booking
- add analytics events for CTA clicks, form starts, form submits

## Week 4: Launch + rapid optimization

- run 2-3 headline tests
- test two CTA labels
- tighten form friction and trust placement
- review session recordings/heatmaps and iterate

2. Keep the page single-purpose: **book qualified demo / ops consult**.
3. Build around your category wedge: **therapeutic antiarrhythmic monitoring service**, not generic remote cardiology AI.
4. Reconcile regulatory status copy immediately so public messaging reflects current FDA reality and intended use constraints.
